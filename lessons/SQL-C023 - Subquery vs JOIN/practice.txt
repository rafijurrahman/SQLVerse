Lesson: SQL-C023 — Subquery vs JOIN
================================

Practice Questions

Question 1:
Find customers with orders using:
(A) JOIN: INNER JOIN customers ON orders.customer_id = customers.customer_id.
(B) Subquery: WHERE customer_id IN (SELECT customer_id FROM orders).

Question 2:
Find total spending per customer using:
(A) JOIN + GROUP BY: JOIN orders, GROUP BY customer_id, SUM(total_amount).
(B) Correlated subquery in SELECT: (SELECT SUM FROM orders WHERE customer_id = c.customer_id).

Question 3:
Find the top-spending customer:
(A) JOIN + GROUP BY + ORDER BY + LIMIT 1.
(B) Subquery: WHERE customer_id = (SELECT customer_id FROM orders GROUP BY customer_id ORDER BY SUM(total_amount) DESC LIMIT 1).

Question 4:
Find customers who spent above average:
(A) JOIN approach.
(B) Subquery approach.
Write both and compare. JOINs are often faster but subqueries can be more readable.
