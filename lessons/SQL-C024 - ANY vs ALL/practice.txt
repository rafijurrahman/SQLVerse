Lesson: SQL-C024 — ANY vs ALL
================================

Practice Questions

Question 1:
Write two queries:
(A) WHERE revenue > ANY (SELECT revenue FROM sales WHERE region = 'Sylhet').
(B) WHERE revenue > ALL (SELECT revenue FROM sales WHERE region = 'Sylhet').
Count how many rows each returns.

Question 2:
ANY means "greater than at least one value" -- equivalent to > MIN().
ALL means "greater than every value" -- equivalent to > MAX().
Write both with their MIN/MAX equivalents and confirm they produce the same results.

Question 3:
Sales targets:
(A) ANY: sales that beat at least one Stationery sale (low bar).
(B) ALL: sales that beat every Stationery sale (high bar).
Display how many records satisfy each condition.

Question 4:
Write < ANY and < ALL comparisons:
(A) < ANY: revenue less than the maximum (almost everything).
(B) < ALL: revenue less than the minimum (very few rows).
Verify with MIN/MAX equivalents.
