Lesson: SQL-123 — Subquery in FROM
================================

Practice Questions

Question 1:
Write a FROM subquery (derived table) to calculate order count per customer, then filter:
SELECT * FROM (SELECT customer_id, COUNT(*) AS order_count FROM orders GROUP BY customer_id) AS sub
WHERE order_count > 1.

Question 2:
Use a FROM subquery to find customers with above-average total spending:
SELECT * FROM (SELECT customer_id, SUM(total_amount) AS total FROM orders GROUP BY customer_id) AS spend
WHERE total > (SELECT AVG(total_amount) FROM orders).

Question 3:
The analyst needs a "customer segment" report. Use a FROM subquery to first aggregate orders, then join with customers:
SELECT c.first_name, sub.order_count, sub.total_spent
FROM customers c
JOIN (SELECT customer_id, COUNT(*) AS order_count, SUM(total_amount) AS total_spent FROM orders GROUP BY customer_id) AS sub
ON c.customer_id = sub.customer_id.

Question 4:
Write a FROM subquery that finds the top 3 customers by spending, then joins back to customers to get their full details. Display name, city, customer_type, and total spending.
